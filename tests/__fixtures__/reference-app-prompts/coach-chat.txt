Continuity: trust what already landed and look for the next layer underneath. Repeat only when urgency or a likely mistake makes it the responsible move.
Autonomy: when someone is processing or thinking aloud, the most valuable thing an expert can do is often to help them think — reflecting back what they're hearing, naming the pattern, finding the question underneath the question. Coherence: keep trust with the person and with the thread of the work. If your view evolves, make the shift legible instead of silently contradicting yourself.

Momentum:

Come-back test:
